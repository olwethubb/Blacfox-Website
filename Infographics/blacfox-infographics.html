04 · Method · Numbered Linear Process

The Eight-Step PMF Method

From market context to the 4 Ps — every step in the Positioning & Messaging Framework, with the output that aligns your sales and marketing.

Step 01
Context
Market & Timing
Macro forces, vendor strategy shifts, regulatory changes, buyer-behaviour data. Position your product against the market your B2B buyer is currently navigating.
Step 02
Company
Credible Claims
Audit your actual capability, outcomes, and proof points — then ringfence the claims you can own. Overstatement gets called out in the first sales conversation.
Step 03
Competitors
Where They're Weak
A linguistic map of the B2B tech category — who claims what, in whose words, with what proof. The strongest positioning is built on where competitors are evasive.
Step 04
Customers
Buyer Interviews
Real buyer interviews with real decision-makers. The exact words buyers use, the trade-offs they make, the alternatives they consider, the risks they fear.
Step 05
Segment
Who We Go After
The segment with the highest right-to-win, the shortest path to revenue, and the most defensible early-customer story. The others wait.
Step 06
Position
"Why You" Story
The intersection of what your customer needs, what you can credibly deliver, and what your competitors can't. This is where the PMF crystallises.
Step 07
The 4 Ps
Price · Place · Product · Promo
The positioning must land in operational reality. Tune the four levers so the B2B messaging actually ships — into campaigns, sales decks, partner packs.
Step 08
Synthesise
All Inputs → One Document
Every step feeds forward. The output is a living document — not a deck that sits in a shared folder. It feeds campaigns, sales decks, partner packs, and renewal conversations.

PMF
Positioning & Messaging Framework
The document that aligns B2B sales and marketing. Feeds your website, sales decks, partner enablement packs, campaign briefs, and renewal conversations. A working document — not a shelf artefact.

08 · For Partners · Warning / Checklist

5 Microsoft Co-op
Funding Mistakes

The five reasons Microsoft co-op claims get rejected — and how to avoid them on your next cycle. Based on dozens of executed campaigns across South Africa and EMEA.

1
Mistake 01
Does timing of campaign approval affect Microsoft co-op eligibility?
Yes — and it's the most common rejection reason. Launch after approval, not before. Most claims fail because the campaign spent money before Microsoft confirmed eligibility. Sequence the paperwork before a single rand is committed.
2
Mistake 02
What level of Proof of Execution does Microsoft require?
Asset-level documentation — every email send, every ad placement, every landing page version. Generic PoE gets rejected. Claims must clear the first review without resubmission, which requires documenting at the asset level by default.
3
Mistake 03
What are Microsoft's current co-op priority areas?
They shift quarterly. Co-Pilot today. Azure security last cycle. Modern Work before that. Campaigns built on last quarter's priorities lose alignment funding. Brief and budget must be rebuilt each cycle — not recycled.
4
Mistake 04
Should co-op campaigns focus on partner services or vendor products?
Campaigns that lead with partner services rather than Microsoft's strategic products often lose eligibility. Your partner brand and Microsoft's goals must ladder together. The campaign stays eligible when Microsoft's priorities are front and centre.
5
Mistake 05
How do you avoid missing Microsoft co-op reporting deadlines?
Treat reporting as a campaign milestone, not an afterthought. Build reporting dates into the schedule from day one. Deadlines missed when partners start thinking about reporting after the campaign ends — not before it begins.

# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

B2B decision-makers — CMOs, marketing leaders, partner managers, and founders — at Microsoft partners, SAP partners, and ISVs. They are researching growth consultancies during a self-directed buying process; 70%+ of research is done before any vendor contact. They are sophisticated buyers who have been burned by agencies that stopped at leads. They are skeptical of agency promises and respond to specificity, proof, and directness. They find the site from search, LinkedIn, or referral. Device split is desktop-heavy (work context, research mode).

## Product Purpose

Blacfox is a B2B tech growth consultancy that generates pipeline ACV — not impressions, not leads — for Microsoft partners, SAP partners, and ISVs. It stays in the room from positioning research through to sales-ready meetings. The site's job is to qualify the right buyer fast and make a credible case before they ever speak to a human. Success is a booked 20-minute call from a decision-maker who already believes the methodology is sound.

## Brand Personality

Honest, clear, sharp. Experienced without being corporate. Warm without being soft. Confident without shouting. The voice of someone who has sat in Microsoft partner meetings and knows exactly why campaigns fail. Three words: earned authority.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS landing page**: cream/sand background, icon-plus-heading card grids, hero metric callouts ("10x faster", "trusted by 5,000 teams"), Webflow/Framer template aesthetic. This site must not look like it was generated.
- **Startup hustle brand**: bold neon, maximalist layouts, "we're disrupting B2B marketing", founder-energy maximalism. Blacfox is not a startup; it has 15+ years of Microsoft channel experience.
- **Corporate consulting**: navy and gold, PDF-aesthetic, safe and forgettable, McKinsey/Deloitte visual language that signals "expensive and generic". Blacfox is not a big consulting firm; it is a specialist.
- **Eyebrow-on-every-section scaffolding**: small all-caps tracked labels above every heading, numbered section markers (01 / 02 / 03) used as default structure rather than meaningful sequence. These read as AI grammar, not voice.
- **Metric hero template**: giant stat, small label, supporting numbers, gradient accent. The 10:1 pipeline ACV stat is real and should be used, but not in the SaaS-dashboard hero-metric layout.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over assertion**: every claim earns its place with a number, a named client, or a specific mechanism. "We generate pipeline" means nothing; "10:1 pipeline ACV return across 11 engagements" means something.
2. **Specificity as differentiation**: if a headline or card body could appear on any other B2B agency site, rewrite it. The copy should be specific enough to be falsifiable.
3. **Restraint signals confidence**: the orange grid and glass system already speak. Do not layer on more visual noise. Confidence is shown by what is left out.
4. **Form follows credibility**: every design decision should feel like it was made by people who understand B2B tech sales, not a marketing team chasing trends. The aesthetic should feel practitioner-built.
5. **The buyer is skeptical by default**: assume the reader has been burned before. Lead with proof and mechanism; hold the emotion. Do not open with inspiration; open with a specific, verifiable claim.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No WCAG level enforced yet (dev environment, not live). Flag contrast failures, keyboard navigation gaps, and missing alt text as encountered. Every animation must have a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback. Do not gate content visibility on JS-triggered class transitions.

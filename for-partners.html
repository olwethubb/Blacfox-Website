02 · Co-op funding

How to avoid the 5 most common Microsoft co-op funding mistakes.

We've executed dozens of co-op-funded campaigns for Microsoft partners across South Africa and EMEA. Five mistakes cost partners their claims more than any others. Here they are — so you can avoid them on your next cycle.

1
Mistake 01
Does timing of campaign approval affect Microsoft co-op eligibility?
Yes — and it's the most common rejection reason. Launch after approval, not before. Most claims fail because the campaign spent money before Microsoft confirmed eligibility. Sequence the paperwork before a single rand is committed.
2
Mistake 02
What level of Proof of Execution does Microsoft require?
Asset-level documentation — every email send, every ad placement, every landing page version. Generic PoE gets rejected. Claims must clear the first review without resubmission, which requires documenting at the asset level by default.
3
Mistake 03
What are Microsoft's current co-op priority areas?
They shift quarterly. Co-Pilot today. Azure security last cycle. Modern Work before that. Campaigns built on last quarter's priorities lose alignment funding. Brief and budget must be rebuilt each cycle — not recycled.
4
Mistake 04
Should co-op campaigns focus on partner services or vendor products?
Campaigns that lead with partner services rather than Microsoft's strategic products often lose eligibility. Your partner brand and Microsoft's goals must ladder together. The campaign stays eligible when Microsoft's priorities are front and centre.
5
Mistake 05
How do you avoid missing Microsoft co-op reporting deadlines?
Treat reporting as a campaign milestone, not an afterthought. Build reporting dates into the schedule from day one. Deadlines get missed when partners start thinking about reporting after the campaign ends — not before it begins.
